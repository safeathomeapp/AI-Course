# Review Synthesis — T1M03

## Module Under Review
- Module ID: T1M03
- Module title: Understanding AI Strengths and Limitations
- Source module: `modules/foundation/T1M03_understanding_ai_strengths_and_limitations.md`

## Review Sample
- Feedback files reviewed: 14/14
- Reviewer profile source: `reviews/reviewer_profiles.md`
- Reviewer types represented:
  - beginners and non-technical managers
  - developers and experienced AI users
  - educators / enterprise L&D evaluators
  - operations, sales, and business leaders
  - creative/content/marketing professionals
  - international/non-native-English learners
  - compliance / regulated-industry stakeholders
  - neurodivergent workflow-sensitive users

## Repeated Strengths
1. Core reframe (“allocation problem, not just prompting problem”) consistently landed as a high-value insight.
2. Clarity/readability were rated very strongly across nearly all personas, including beginners and non-native-English reviewers.
3. Risk-tier model (low/medium/high + review depth) was widely seen as practical and immediately transferable.
4. “Fluency creates false confidence” and “urgency pushes teams toward auto-accept” were repeatedly cited as the most behavior-changing diagnostics.
5. Guided matrix exercise produced real workplace artefacts and concrete behavior change (not just conceptual understanding).
6. Evaluation checklist and reflection prompts (especially over-trust identification) were repeatedly reported as high-impact.
7. Key principle (“AI supports; humans retain authority”) was widely described as policy-ready language.

## Repeated Concerns
1. Demonstrations are clear but domain coverage is too narrow for broad professional audiences.
2. Medium-risk and ambiguous-risk classification needs more calibration guidance.
3. Team-level implementation guidance is limited relative to how often learners need to operationalize this in groups.
4. Regulated-industry documentation/compliance implications are underrepresented for high-stakes deployments.
5. Some learners still need slightly richer matrix exemplars despite strong baseline scaffolding.
6. Limited guidance on maintaining the matrix as a living document over time.

## High-Priority Confirmed Issues
1. `T1M03-01` Demonstration domain-coverage gap remains substantial across persona groups.
2. `T1M03-02` Medium-risk/context-dependent calibration is under-specified.
3. `T1M03-03` Team operationalization layer is not yet strong enough for manager-led deployment.
4. `T1M03-06` Matrix maintenance/versioning guidance is repeatedly requested for real deployment contexts.
5. `T1M03-05` Example depth is still uneven for some beginner and early-career cohorts.

## Possible Issues To Watch
1. `T1M03-04` Regulated-context governance gap (low frequency, high impact).
2. Whether persona-specific adaptation requests grow in later modules (`T1M03-07`) into a broader accessibility signal.

## Outliers / Low-Value Feedback
1. `T1M03-08` Reordering module sequence request (single-reviewer preference).
2. `T1M03-07` Highly specific persona-tailoring asks not supported as broad blockers.
3. `T1M03-09` Business packaging requests beyond this module’s core scope.

## Stability Notes
What should remain unchanged based on repeated strong evidence:
1. Keep the allocation-first framing and opening thesis language.
2. Keep the current clarity style (plain language, short sections, direct professional tone).
3. Keep the three-tier risk model with explicit review-depth mapping.
4. Keep the matrix exercise structure and checklist-reflection flow.
5. Keep the key principle language on human authority over correctness/risk/final decisions.
6. Keep customer-support and operations-style governance examples; they were highly valued by several manager cohorts.

## Revision Priorities
1. Improve demonstration coverage with targeted cross-role variants while preserving concise format.
2. Add explicit calibration aids for medium/ambiguous risk classification.
3. Strengthen team-deployment guidance (enforcement, escalation, ownership clarity).
4. Add optional regulated-context note for compliance-critical environments.
5. Add concise “living document” maintenance guidance for the matrix.
