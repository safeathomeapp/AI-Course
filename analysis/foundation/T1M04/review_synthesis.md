# Review Synthesis — T1M04

## Module Under Review
- Module ID: T1M04
- Module title: Structured Prompting Fundamentals
- Source module: `modules/foundation/T1M04_structured_prompting_fundamentals.md`

## Review Sample
- Feedback files reviewed: 14/14
- Reviewer profile source: `reviews/reviewer_profiles.md`
- Reviewer types represented:
  - beginners and non-technical managers
  - developers and heavy AI users
  - operations, sales, and business leaders
  - educators / enterprise L&D evaluators
  - creative/content/marketing professionals
  - international/non-native-English learners
  - compliance/regulated-industry stakeholders
  - neurodivergent workflow-sensitive users

## Repeated Strengths
1. Module purpose is immediately clear and consistently rated high for accessibility and professional relevance.
2. RCTCO structure (Role, Context, Task, Constraints, Output Format) is memorable and immediately actionable across roles.
3. Failure-mode mapping per element was repeatedly described as the module's strongest teaching mechanism.
4. Guided exercise (real prompt rewrite + before/after test + checklist) produced immediate behavior change and visible output-quality gains.
5. Prompt library artefact was widely viewed as high-value and usable beyond the course (individual and team contexts).
6. Real-world framing remained aligned with prior governance principle: structured prompting improves output quality, human review retains final accountability.
7. Template-style scaffolding reduced startup friction for beginners and executive-function-sensitive learners.

## Repeated Concerns
1. Constraint-writing method is under-taught relative to its importance and relative difficulty.
2. Example/demonstration coverage still feels uneven for several role clusters (dev, sales, research, regulated contexts).
3. Team deployment and governance guidance for prompt libraries is lighter than many managers need.
4. Minor terminology inconsistency and occasional jargon reduce clarity for some cohorts.
5. Prompt library long-term hygiene guidance (organize/maintain/version) is limited.
6. Exercise preconditions (tool access and safe-data context) are implicit rather than explicit.

## High-Priority Confirmed Issues
1. `T1M04-01` Constraint-writing methodology gap (most frequent concern).
2. `T1M04-02` Demonstration role-coverage gap persists across persona groups.
3. `T1M04-03` Team operationalization guidance remains underdeveloped for deployment contexts.
4. `T1M04-04` Terminology consistency issue (`output schema` vs `output format`) recurs across many reviews.

## Possible Issues To Watch
1. `T1M04-05` Tool-access/safe-practice assumptions in exercise instructions.
2. `T1M04-10` Regulated-environment caveats (low frequency, high impact).
3. `T1M04-06` Checklist anchor/rubric consistency for team-scale assessment contexts.

## Outliers / Low-Value Feedback
1. `T1M04-09` Creative-output-format caveat appears as a single-reviewer nuance.
2. `T1M04-08` Iteration/model-variability/chaining depth requests are useful but mostly scope-adjacent preferences rather than clear blockers.

## Stability Notes
What should remain unchanged based on repeated evidence:
1. Keep the opening thesis and overall direct, practical framing.
2. Keep the five-part RCTCO structure as the module's central control model.
3. Keep the failure-mode mapping per element; this is repeatedly the top retention driver.
4. Keep the real-prompt comparison exercise and evaluation checklist structure.
5. Keep the prompt library artefact concept and Review Notes field.
6. Keep explicit governance continuity with T1M03 (human review owns final quality).

## Revision Priorities
1. Add explicit, transferable method for writing constraints from task-specific failure modes.
2. Broaden practical example coverage with concise role-variant cases for under-served audiences.
3. Add lightweight team deployment guidance for prompt-library governance.
4. Normalize terminology and simplify minor jargon where clarity gains are easy.
5. Add concise guidance for prompt-library maintenance/organization and exercise setup assumptions.
