## Core Principles

### AI Is Not an Authority

AI produces probabilistic outputs, not guaranteed truth.

Its role is to support thinking, drafting, analysis, and workflow execution. It is not the final owner of correctness, judgment, or decisions.

### Verification Is Required

Important claims, interpretations, and outputs must be checked before use.

Verification should include, where relevant:

- checking facts
- questioning assumptions
- identifying unknowns
- confirming fitness for purpose

### Humans Remain Accountable

Responsibility always remains with the human user, reviewer, or decision-maker.

The core operating model remains:

AI drafts. Humans review. Humans decide.

### Use Structured Instructions

Professional AI use should rely on clear instructions rather than vague requests.

At minimum, good AI use should define:

- role
- context
- task
- constraints
- output format

### Use AI Within Workflows

AI is most useful when treated as part of a structured workflow rather than a one-step replacement for judgment.

Typical workflow stages may include:

- research
- summarization
- analysis
- drafting
- review

### Avoid Blind Automation

Users should understand a process before trying to automate it.

Fluent output is not the same as reliable output.

### Focus on Systems, Not Tricks

The durable skill is not novelty prompting. It is designing repeatable, reviewable, responsible workflows.

## Final Principle

This course should consistently teach and model responsible AI use:

- AI can be highly useful
- AI can also be confidently wrong
- humans must verify outputs
- humans remain responsible for outcomes
