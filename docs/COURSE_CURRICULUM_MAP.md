# COURSE_CURRICULUM_MAP.md

## Project

An AI's Guide for Humans: Understanding and Harnessing Artificial
Intelligence

This document maps the entire curriculum across the three course tiers.
It is the authoritative reference for module planning and development.

Total Program Structure

Tier 1 --- Foundations of AI (≈20 hours) Tier 2 --- Applied AI Workflows
(≈20--30 hours) Tier 3 --- AI Systems & Architecture (≈30--40 hours)

Each module follows the standard module template defined in
COURSE_MODULE_TEMPLATE.md.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Tier 1 --- Foundations of AI

Estimated Duration: 20 Hours

Purpose: Introduce learners to how AI systems behave and how to control
outputs effectively.

## Module List

Module 1.1 --- Introduction to AI as a Tool Concept: AI as a
professional instrument rather than a novelty. Outcome: Learners
understand the philosophy of the course.

Module 1.2 --- How Large Language Models Actually Work Concept: tokens,
probability, training data, context windows. Outcome: Learners
understand why hallucinations occur.

Module 1.3 --- Understanding AI Strengths and Limitations Concept:
pattern recognition vs factual reliability. Outcome: Learners know when
AI should and should not be trusted.

Module 1.4 --- Structured Prompting Fundamentals Concept: Role → Context
→ Task → Constraints → Output. Outcome: Learners produce predictable
outputs.

Module 1.5 --- Controlling Output Structure Concept: forcing AI to
return tables, lists, JSON structures. Outcome: structured results for
workflows.

Module 1.6 --- AI for Research and Knowledge Exploration Concept:
AI-assisted discovery and summarisation.

Module 1.7 --- AI as a Thinking Partner Concept: debate prompting,
critique loops.

Module 1.8 --- Avoiding Common AI Mistakes Concept: hallucinations,
vague prompts, blind trust.

Module 1.9 --- Building Your First AI Workflow Concept: chaining prompts
into a small workflow.

Module 1.10 --- Foundations Portfolio Project Learners produce: - prompt
library - workflow example - AI-assisted research report

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Tier 2 --- Applied AI Workflows

Estimated Duration: 20--30 Hours

Purpose: Move from isolated AI prompts to repeatable professional
workflows.

## Module List

Module 2.1 --- Workflow Thinking Concept: breaking tasks into stages.

Module 2.2 --- Prompt Chains and Process Pipelines Concept: multi-step
prompt architecture.

Module 2.3 --- AI for Professional Communication Concept: executive
summaries, reports, structured communication.

Module 2.4 --- AI for Analysis and Decision Support Concept: SWOT, risk
matrices, decision frameworks.

Module 2.5 --- AI for Documentation and SOP Creation Concept: converting
knowledge into structured processes.

Module 2.6 --- AI for Data Interpretation Concept: extracting insights
from structured information.

Module 2.7 --- AI for Knowledge Management Concept: organising
information with AI support.

Module 2.8 --- Workflow Automation Concepts Concept: integrating AI with
tools and processes.

Module 2.9 --- AI-Assisted Productivity Systems Concept: task pipelines
and productivity frameworks.

Module 2.10 --- Applied Portfolio Project Learners produce: -
AI-enhanced SOP - workflow library - decision analysis framework

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Tier 3 --- AI Systems & Architecture

Estimated Duration: 30--40 Hours

Purpose: Teach learners to design AI-powered tools, assistants, and
systems.

## Module List

Module 3.1 --- From Workflows to Systems Concept: designing AI
architectures.

Module 3.2 --- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) Concept: connecting
AI to external knowledge sources.

Module 3.3 --- Knowledge Assistants Concept: internal documentation
assistants.

Module 3.4 --- AI Agents and Task Automation Concept: multi-step
autonomous workflows.

Module 3.5 --- Human-in-the-Loop Design Concept: oversight and
accountability.

Module 3.6 --- AI System Reliability Concept: testing, monitoring, and
evaluation.

Module 3.7 --- Designing AI Products Concept: identifying product
opportunities.

Module 3.8 --- Integrating AI with Business Processes Concept: embedding
AI into operational systems.

Module 3.9 --- Governance and Risk Management Concept: safe and
responsible deployment.

Module 3.10 --- Capstone Project Learners design: - AI assistant
system - workflow architecture - prototype AI tool

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Portfolio Outcome Across the Entire Program

Learners completing all tiers will possess:

• Prompt engineering library • AI workflow documentation • AI-assisted
SOP examples • decision frameworks • AI system architecture proposal

These artefacts demonstrate practical capability to employers or
collaborators.
