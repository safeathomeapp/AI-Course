# COURSE_PHILOSOPHY.md

## Purpose

This document defines the guiding philosophy of the AI-Course.

It establishes the principles that shape how the course teaches AI usage and how learners are expected to approach AI in real work.

The philosophy should remain stable even as tools, models, and workflows evolve.

## Core Philosophy

Artificial intelligence is not an authority.
It is a tool.

This course exists to help people understand how AI behaves before they rely on it in real work.

The central principle is:

AI drafts. Humans review. Humans decide.

---

## Central Model

Prompt -> Context -> Generation -> Human Review -> Decision

AI proposes.
Humans decide.

---

## Why This Course Exists

Many AI courses sit at one of two extremes:

1. shallow prompt tricks
2. deep machine learning theory

This course is built for the practical space between those extremes.

It is designed to help learners use AI deliberately, responsibly, and effectively in professional contexts.

---

## Learner Outcome Intent

Learners should leave the course able to:

- use AI safely in professional work
- design repeatable AI workflows
- evaluate reliability and risk
- integrate AI into real decision processes
