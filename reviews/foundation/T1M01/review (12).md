# 10. Weakest Part

The demonstrations — specifically the choice of operations and finance examples to the exclusion of sales, and the absence of any ROI or business impact framing.

**Why:** Two reasons.

First, relevance. My primary concern is whether this course will drive behavior change in a B2B sales team. The demonstrations are where that connection should be made, and it isn't. An onboarding documentation example and a P&L summary example are not proximate enough to a sales rep's daily workflow to close the "so what?" gap without additional effort. If deployed as-is, I'd expect significant "this doesn't apply to me" feedback from reps, which creates adoption risk and therefore ROI risk.

Second, measurement. There's no indication of what productivity improvement a learner can expect from applying this framework. I cannot build a business case for 85 licenses without benchmarks. "Better outputs" is not a CFO conversation. "Estimated 3-4 hours saved per rep per week on research and drafting" is a CFO conversation. That data doesn't exist in this module.
