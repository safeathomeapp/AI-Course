# 2. Concept Explanation

**Did the explanation help you understand the concept?**

Yes, it did help. The main idea — that AI makes output from probability, and human must review before making decision — I understood this. It is similar to how I already think about using AI in my work: I use AI to make draft, then I check and revise.

**Did any terminology feel confusing?**

Yes, I want to be specific because I think this is helpful feedback.

"Context window" — I did not know this phrase. The explanation say "the model can only use what is in scope." But "in scope" is also phrase I am not familiar with easily. I had to look up both phrases. I think definition like "context window = the information you give to AI in one conversation" would be clearer.

"Tokens" — this word appear in sentence "the model predicts likely next tokens." I know "token" in other contexts (security token, transportation token) but not in AI context. The module did not explain this. I looked in dictionary and then in Google Translate but still not 100% clear. Is it similar to "word"? Or different?

"Probability engine" — I understand each word. But "engine" here is metaphor, yes? In Japanese we might say "仕組み" (mechanism) not "engine" for this concept. Not wrong, but requires interpretation.

"Hallucination" — this is word I know from medical context (seeing things that are not real). Using it for AI mistakes is interesting but strange at first. The explanation was helpful so I did understand finally. But for non-native speaker, this metaphor takes extra thought.

Also I want to mention one sentence that was very difficult for me to understand quickly:
