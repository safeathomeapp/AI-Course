# 9. Most Valuable Part

**What was the single most useful idea in this module?**

The six-step mental model — Prompt → Context Window → Probability Engine → Output → Human Review → Decision — combined with making Human Review an explicit mandatory step.

**Why:**

For my ADHD brain, having a named, sequential framework to hold in my head is genuinely life-changing. Without it I'm reacting to AI output intuitively — sometimes reviewing carefully, sometimes not, depending on my focus state that day. With a framework, the review step is part of the sequence, not an optional add-on I might remember to do.

This also connects directly to my ADHD AI use: I often use AI as an external scaffold when my executive function is low. The irony is that when my executive function is low is exactly when I'm least likely to review the output carefully. Having this model makes the review step explicit even in low-function moments: "I'm on step 5 now. Do the review. Don't skip it."

The framework also helps me notice when I'm in the wrong pattern. "Oh, I just went from Output to Decision without a Human Review step. That's the failure pattern. Catch it."

Second weakest: the hallucination paragraph in section 2. Important content, inaccessible formatting. Short paragraphs and a summary callout would fix it.
