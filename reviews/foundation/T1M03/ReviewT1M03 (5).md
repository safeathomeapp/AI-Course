# 10. Weakest Part

The demonstrations' domain coverage. Product management and customer support are both valid illustrations of the principle. For a marketing-heavy course audience, neither is the immediate professional analogue.

I also think the where-AI-is-strong/weak lists could be more nuanced — "first-draft writing" as a single low-risk category doesn't capture the range within that category. A first draft of internal meeting notes is different from a first draft of a CEO's public-facing thought leadership piece. Both are "first-draft writing." They're not both low-risk.

---

# 11. Missing Content

Module is well-structured and self-contained.

What would significantly improve it for marketing and team contexts:

1. **Marketing or growth function demonstration.** As above.

2. **Team deployment guidance.** How do you roll out a task-allocation protocol to a marketing team? What does enforcement look like without creating bureaucratic overhead? I'm going to figure this out through the workshop I'm running, but the module should address this.

3. **Multi-tool risk consideration.** Different AI tools have different reliability profiles. The allocation framework treats AI as monolithic. Some guidance on how to account for tool-specific reliability would be valuable for heavy AI users who work across multiple platforms.

4. **The "first-draft writing" category needs unpacking.** Stakes vary dramatically within this category and the module doesn't capture that.
