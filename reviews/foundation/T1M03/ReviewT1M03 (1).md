# 9. Most Valuable Part

The "why people misallocate" section.

Specifically: "fluency creates false confidence." This is the most practically important insight in the module and possibly in the course so far. Understanding that polished AI output is not a signal of verified accuracy — that teams assume the quality of presentation reflects the quality of content — is the psychological root of most AI misuse in professional settings. Naming it and explaining it gives teams the language to recognize the dynamic and resist it.

This is something I'm going to reference in every AI discussion I have at work going forward.

The before/after prompts in the product management demo in particular feel a bit abstract. # 11. Missing Content

The module assumes knowledge of:
- What prompts are (reasonable, covered in Module 1.1)
- Basic AI tool interface familiarity (reasonable)

What's actually missing:
1. **Data analysis and reporting examples.** Ops analysts, financial analysts, and data professionals use AI heavily for analytical work. The entire demonstration section uses writing and decision-making scenarios. Where are the data-heavy use cases?

2. **A "cost of error" column in the base matrix template.** This is mentioned as an optional add-on but should be standard for anyone using AI in consequential professional work.

3. **Guidance on what to do when task risk is ambiguous.** Some tasks don't fit neatly into low/medium/high — they're context-dependent. How do you classify a task that's low-risk in normal conditions but high-risk during month-end close or audit periods?

4. **Examples of the matrix in use over time.** How do you maintain and update the matrix as AI capabilities change? A matrix built today might need revision in six months. Some guidance on treating this as a living document would be useful.
