# 11. Missing Content

Module is self-contained and accessible. No missing prerequisites.

Suggested additions:

1. **UX research or design research demonstration.** Qualitative data synthesis is a specific AI use case with specific risks (pattern fabrication, thin evidence, interpretive authority) that should be addressed for the research professional audience.

2. **Neurodivergent-specific vulnerability note.** The module correctly identifies that urgency and fluency-confidence are misallocation risk factors. For ADHD learners, both of these are heightened risk factors — urgency is more impactful on low executive function days, and hyperfocus on interesting AI output increases confidence-without-verification risk. A brief acknowledgment would help neurodivergent learners prepare specifically.

3. **Risk variability over time.** My own insight from the exercise: some tasks are medium-risk in normal conditions but high-risk in certain states (low energy, time pressure, incomplete briefing). Static risk classification doesn't fully capture this. Some guidance on dynamic risk assessment would be useful.

4. **Mid-process risk.** The module focuses on final output risk. Intermediate steps (like my affinity diagram labeling example) can also carry risk when they feed into high-stakes final outputs. This dimension deserves a note.
