# Module 1.6 AI for Research and Knowledge Exploration

## Module Overview

**Tier:** Foundations  
**Estimated Duration:** 75-120 minutes  
**Prerequisites:** Module 1.2 and Module 1.4

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

- use AI to accelerate early-stage research and synthesis
- separate source-backed facts from interpretation
- produce clear knowledge summaries with explicit open questions

---

## 1. Concept Explanation

AI is effective for exploring unfamiliar topics quickly, but research quality depends on source quality and verification discipline.

Why this matters:
- knowledge work often starts with large amounts of material
- AI can compress reading and surface themes fast
- unverified synthesis can spread errors

How AI behavior relates to this concept:
- AI can summarize patterns across provided text
- AI may fill gaps with plausible but unsupported claims
- explicit "facts vs interpretation" instructions reduce confusion

Where learners commonly go wrong:
- asking for summaries without providing source text
- merging speculative commentary with factual statements
- skipping uncertainty and open-question tracking

Short example: Product discovery research should separate direct user quotes from inferred product opportunities.

---

## 2. Demonstration

### Example Context
Product management: summarize user interview notes for a feature decision.

### Weak Example
"Read these notes and tell me what to build."

### Improved Example
"You are a research synthesis assistant.

Context:
I have 12 user interview summaries about onboarding friction.

Task:
Summarize the most important findings and decision implications.

Constraints:
- Separate direct evidence from interpretation
- Do not invent user quotes
- Highlight uncertainty where evidence is weak

Output Format:
1. Top findings (evidence-based)
2. Inferred opportunities
3. Risks and assumptions
4. Open research questions"

### Analysis
The improved prompt enforces evidence separation and uncertainty tracking. It supports decision preparation while keeping humans accountable for final interpretation.

## 4. Real-World Application

Research workflows improve in:
- finance: quick synthesis of policy updates before deeper review
- software: documentation review and architecture orientation
- product: interview and feedback theme extraction
- marketing: competitor messaging analysis
- operations: process issue synthesis from incident notes
- support: recurring complaint pattern identification

---

## 5. Common Mistakes

- not defining the research question
- failing to separate evidence from inference
- accepting summaries without source checks
- omitting open questions

These mistakes produce polished output with weak reliability.
